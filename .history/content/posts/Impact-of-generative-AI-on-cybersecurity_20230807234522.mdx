Telesign presents an in-depth discussion on the growing threats to personal data in the digital realm and the need for a comprehensive, multi-layered fraud prevention approach. As fraud prevention technology advances, account takeover (ATO) attacks have risen by 307% between 2019 and 2021, resulting in staggering losses of $11.4 billion, along with damage to credibility and customer trust.
<br/>
The surge in ATO attacks is facilitated by massive data breaches, social engineering, and AI-powered phishing and brute force attacks. Generative AI poses a new threat, enabling attackers to create convincing deep fakes, such as simulating voices, leading to instances of blackmail and breaking authentication schemes. However, it also offers opportunities for defenders to automate suspicious behavior monitoring.
<br/>
To counter cyber threats, industry experts emphasize the importance of raising awareness and investing in robust technology, like multifactor authentication and password vaults. Educating employees and consumers is crucial in preventing fraud. Consumers should proactively implement a multi-layered approach to safeguard their accounts and remain vigilant about website credentials, suspicious emails, and messages.
<br/>
The webinar also covers how mobile identity can defend against fraud, advanced security protocols and strategies, and the need for ongoing efforts to improve cybersecurity through distributed networks and verifiable credentials. The shared goal is to combat cybercrime and protect personal data from relentless attacks in the digital age.

# ***The Cloud Company at the Center of a Global Hacking Spree***
<br/>
<Image src="/The-Cloud-Company-at-the-Center-of-a-Global-Hacking-Spree-Security-GettyImages-1484224583.jpeg" alt="" width={600} height={500} />
<br />
In 2024, the United States is facing a chaotic and nightmare-inducing scenario. Indictments against former president Donald Trump, coupled with a tumultuous election season where Trump plays a prominent role, contribute to the overall unrest. The rapid advancement of generative artificial intelligence (AI) technology brings about a rise in personalized disinformation, with tools like ChatGPT and Google's Bard enabling more sophisticated and targeted manipulation of information. Regulating AI poses significant challenges, given Congress's struggles with privacy legislation.
<br/>
Security researchers are uncovering new ways to bypass the safeguards of generative AI tools, such as "adversarial attacks," where attaching seemingly random instructions can manipulate the AI's output. This vulnerability needs addressing to prevent malicious use.
<br/>
Beyond AI concerns, cybersecurity vulnerabilities continue to persist in various platforms. For instance, the Points platform, used by numerous travel rewards programs, recently faced security flaws that exposed users' private information and allowed potential attackers to gain unlimited rewards points. While the bugs have been patched, they underscore the ongoing need for vigilant security measures.
<br/>
Cloudzy, a cloud services provider, was found to have served at least 17 state-sponsored hacking groups, including those from China, Russia, North Korea, and other countries. Additionally, the infamous hacker group Cult of the Dead Cow (cDc) has developed Veilid, a framework for encrypting social media, promising enhanced privacy protections for users.
<br/>
Microsoft disclosed that state-backed hackers linked to Russia executed highly targeted phishing attacks using compromised Microsoft 365 accounts to dupe users through Microsoft Teams messages. These hackers are believed to be part of the group APT29 or Cozy Bear, associated with Russia's Foreign Intelligence Service.
<br/>
Furthermore, a couple was arrested in 2022 for stealing and laundering $4.5 billion in Bitcoin from the Bitfinex exchange in 2016. They have now pleaded guilty to various charges related to the hack, shedding light on the mystery surrounding the exchange's security issues.
<br/>
Amidst these security and privacy challenges, it becomes crucial for both governments and technology companies to work together in developing effective regulations and robust security measures to protect individuals and organizations from potential harm.

# ***The top 10 technologies defining the future of cybersecurity***
<br/>
<Image src="/1-Lead-story-Why-so-many-organizations-are-getting-zero-trust-wrong.jpeg" alt="" width={600} height={500} />
<br />
CISOs (Chief Information Security Officers) are facing the challenge of safeguarding new digital transformation strategies while securing legacy systems and battling identity-related threats with limited cybersecurity staff. To address these challenges, many organizations are adopting a consolidation strategy that leverages advancements in AI and machine learning (ML) to strengthen cybersecurity platforms.
<br/>
The consolidation approach offers benefits such as increased visibility and control, cost savings, and scalability. Gartner's survey reveals that three-quarters of security professionals are actively pursuing a vendor consolidation strategy, with data security platforms, cloud native application protection platforms, identity and access management, extended detection and response, and secure access service edge being the top areas of focus.
<br/>
AI and ML have become essential components of cybersecurity, helping process vast amounts of data for threat detection and classification. Leading cybersecurity vendors have integrated AI into their platforms, offering real-time detection and automated response capabilities. Technologies like endpoint detection and response, endpoint protection platforms, extended detection and response, identity threat detection and response, mobile threat defense, microsegmentation, secure access service edge, secure service edge, unified endpoint security, and zero-trust network access are proving effective in meeting CISOs' challenges.
<br/>
By adopting these technologies, organizations aim to streamline cybersecurity efforts, reduce risk, and protect against internal and external threats while benefiting from AI-driven enhancements and improving their security postures.

# ***Protecting data in the era of generative AI: Nightfall AI launches innovative security platform***
<br/>
<Image src="/nuneybits_vector_art_of_a_double_helix_spiral_made_of_glowing__0635169d-fcb5-425e-b8b1-27d569da5f8d.jpeg" alt="" width={600} height={500} />
<br />
As organizations seek to harness the productivity gains of generative AI, particularly ChatGPT, the security risk of confidential data leakage into large language models (LLMs) becomes a significant concern. Chief Information Security Officers (CISOs) are divided on how to handle this issue, making AI governance a prominent topic in risk management discussions with boards of directors.
<br/>
Some healthcare CISOs and CIOs have opted to restrict ChatGPT access across certain business units to protect sensitive data. However, completely forgoing generative AI research tools puts these organizations at a competitive disadvantage, despite the risks to intellectual property, pricing, and licensing.
<br/>
In response to the productivity paradox and security challenges surrounding gen AI adoption, Nightfall AI has introduced a data security platform designed specifically for gen AI. The platform consists of three products:
<br/>
1. Nightfall for ChatGPT: A browser-based solution that provides real-time scanning and redaction of sensitive data entered into chatbots, minimizing impact on users' experiences.
<br/>
2. Nightfall for LLMs: A developer API that detects and redacts data used to train LLMs, offering customizability and scalability to protect sensitive information.
<br/>
3. Nightfall for SaaS: Provides data leak prevention within popular SaaS applications, enabling companies to use third-party AI systems while maintaining control over sensitive data.
<br/>
Nightfall AI's platform aims to strike a balance between unlocking productivity gains and reducing the risk of data breaches. The availability of these gen AI-focused solutions marks an important milestone in realizing AI's potential while ensuring data security.
