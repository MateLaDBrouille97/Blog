Nat Bullard [Twitter](https://twitter.com/NatBullard), an energy and climate analyst, shares his annual presentation on decarbonization, covering the past, present, and future of energy, transport, industry, technology, and financial systems.
<br/>
<Image src="/public/energy_demande.png" alt="" width={600} height={500} />

Primary energy consumption in OECD countries peaked in 2007, while ex-OECD countries surpassed them in primary energy consumption.
Wind and solar power added 600-700 terawatt-hours of new generation in 2022, which is more than natural gas has ever added in a year and twice as much as nuclear added at its peak in the mid-1980s.

<br/>
<Image src="/public/car_sales.png" alt="" width={600} height={500} />
Electric vehicle (EV) sales have grown from less than one million to more than 10 million, and are the only growth area in the passenger car industry.
China's passenger car exports exceeded 2.5 million last year, putting them close to Germany as an auto exporter.

<br/>
<Image src="/public/Hard-industrie.png" alt="" width={600} height={500} />
Decarbonizing industries such as aluminum, cement, and steel is a significant challenge due to their reliance on coal and human capital is needed to meet net zero targets.
There is a lack of energy expertise in global boardrooms, with only 1.2% of Fortune 100 board members having any qualification in energy and 0.2% having expertise in climate.
