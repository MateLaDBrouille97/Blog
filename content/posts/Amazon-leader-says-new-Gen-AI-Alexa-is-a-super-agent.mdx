<br/>
Amazon's generative AI leader, Rohit Prasad, has declared that Alexa has become a "super agent" following the integration of a new large language model (LLM) into the platform. This LLM is now connected to a vast array of devices and services through numerous APIs, enabling Alexa to provide real-time, useful knowledge directly to users. Prasad compared this development to the momentous launch of Alexa in 2014.
<br/>
He emphasized that Amazon's goal has always been to create a personal AI that can interact naturally and perform tasks on users' behalf. Unlike chatbots like ChatGPT, Alexa focuses on real-world utility, bridging the gap between AI's creativity and practical applications.
<br/>
The new Alexa model is multimodal, multilingual, and multifaceted, incorporating computer vision, natural language processing, and pattern recognition. Prasad addressed previous criticisms of Alexa's usefulness and highlighted its massive user base and growth in interactions.
<br/>
Regarding privacy concerns, Prasad emphasized the importance of customer trust and transparency in data collection. He stated that Alexa is an AI, and while it now possesses more human-like capabilities, it should never be forgotten that it remains an artificial intelligence.
<br/>
In summary, Amazon's integration of a state-of-the-art LLM into Alexa marks a significant step forward, positioning Alexa as a powerful "super agent" capable of delivering real-world utility to users, while maintaining a strong focus on privacy and transparency.

# ***Microsoft announces AI Copilot for Windows coming September 26th***
<br/>
Microsoft has announced a significant expansion of its AI capabilities, integrating its AI companion Copilot into the upcoming version of Windows 11, set to release on September 26, 2023. Copilot, developed in partnership with OpenAI, will be deeply embedded within the operating system, enabling it to work seamlessly across various applications. One notable feature is "Sound Like Me," where Copilot can analyze a user's writing style and compose emails in Microsoft Outlook. This integration extends to classic Microsoft apps like Paint, Photos, Word, and Excel, which will now generate content based on user instructions.
<br/>
Microsoft envisions Copilot as a personal assistant that can access information across devices. For instance, it can pull details from texts or emails, such as airplane ticket information, and provide them through a mobile app or Windows itself. This cross-device functionality aims to enhance productivity and user convenience.
<br/>
Additionally, a Microsoft 365 Copilot for enterprise customers will launch on November 1, 2023, offering features like summarizing work emails and real-time market research. A Copilot Lab will enable users to learn prompt engineering.
<br/>
Microsoft's Bing search engine has also seen improvements, with a billion chats conducted since its launch earlier this year. Bing now includes Microsoft Shopping, offering promotional codes and personalized results based on users' preferences and previous chats.
<br/>
Furthermore, Microsoft's Bing Image Creator, powered by OpenAI's DALL-E 3, has been upgraded to generate images based on natural language descriptions, enhancing image understanding.
<br/>
Overall, Microsoft is leveraging its AI capabilities to deeply integrate Copilot into its ecosystem, enhancing user experiences and productivity across various devices and applications.

# ***AI-assisted cybersecurity: 3 key components you can’t ignore***
<br/>
The article, presented by Zscaler, discusses the profound impact of artificial intelligence (AI), specifically Language Model Models (LLMs) like OpenAI's ChatGPT, on cybersecurity and its potential implications for the future. It begins by drawing a parallel with Garry Kasparov's experience playing against IBM's Deep Blue in chess, highlighting how AI-assisted humans initially had an edge in certain domains.
<br/>
The article outlines three key aspects at the intersection of cybersecurity and AI:
<br/>
1. **Security of AI**: Companies are compelled to adopt AI to remain competitive. However, they must address issues like privacy, intellectual property, governance, ethical usage, data sourcing controls, and access controls. An ethical use review board is proposed to monitor AI applications for correct and ethical use.
<br/>
2. **AI in Defense**: AI can enhance cybersecurity by automating rote tasks, improving efficiency, and assisting human analysts. The emphasis is on process efficiencies, data extrapolation, and incident response. The importance of maintaining human oversight and creativity is stressed.
<br/>
3. **AI in Offense**: The article acknowledges that adversaries also use AI, necessitating the transformation of security practices. Red teams, blue teams, GRC (Governance, Risk Management, and Compliance), data protection, identity, and access management, and deception technologies are cited as areas where AI can be employed to counteract threats.
<br/>
The authors emphasize that we are entering an era of AI-assisted human triumph, rather than AI dominance over humans. To address this, they suggest the need for guidelines and strategies to harness AI's potential effectively. While acknowledging short-term challenges, such as improved phishing and malware attacks, the article is optimistic about the long-term benefits of AI in cybersecurity defense.
<br/>
In conclusion, the article underscores the importance of AI in shaping the future of cybersecurity and encourages businesses and security professionals to embrace AI while maintaining vigilance and ethical practices. It suggests that AI will be a critical tool in the ongoing battle against cyber threats.

# ***Oracle brings voice-activated AI to healthcare with Clinical Digital Assistant***
<br/>
Oracle has announced its integration of an AI-powered Clinical Digital Assistant into its electronic health record (EHR) solutions at the VB Transform 2023 conference. This move aims to assist healthcare providers in automating administrative tasks, allowing them to focus more on patient care. This development is particularly significant as the healthcare sector has been cautious in adopting generative AI due to concerns about bias in AI outputs.
<br/>
Oracle's Clinical Digital Assistant will address staffing issues in healthcare, which are expected to worsen, with a projected shortage of 18 million workers by 2030. The AI assistant will enhance the self-service experience for patients and improve workflow efficiency for clinicians.
<br/>
The AI-powered assistant will work via text or voice commands, enabling clinicians to access patient information and data during appointments without the need to interact with a screen. It can retrieve various types of information from the database, including images and documents, and provide them to the physician in a relevant order. Furthermore, the assistant remains active throughout the appointment, handling administrative tasks such as note-taking and suggesting context-aware actions like medication orders or lab scheduling.
<br/>
Patients can also benefit from the Clinical Digital Assistant by using it to schedule appointments, pay bills, or seek medical information through natural language queries. Providers can integrate it with their secure portals to offer helpful information and reminders.
<br/>
Oracle plans a gradual rollout of these capabilities over the next 12 months. This initiative is part of Oracle's broader effort to incorporate generative AI into its services, with previous efforts including AI features for human capital management and the development of a cloud service for customized large language models. Overall, Oracle aims to improve healthcare processes, reduce clinician burnout, and enhance patient experiences through the adoption of AI technology in healthcare.

# ***YouTube to add AI creator tools to find music for videos, add dubs***
<br/>
YouTube is enhancing its Creator Music feature with the integration of new AI capabilities and introducing an AI-powered dubbing tool. Originally launched last year, Creator Music enables content creators to search for songs, artists, or music genres to include in their videos. With the upcoming expansion, YouTube plans to introduce a music concierge feature in early next year. Creators can input descriptions of the type and length of music they need, and the Creator Music tool will suggest suitable tracks, streamlining the selection process.
<br/>
The Creator Music dashboard initially provided creators with the ability to search for music based on their preferences, collections, genres, and associated licensing costs. It also allowed creators to set budget limits for their projects, offering options to purchase a license or enter into a revenue-sharing agreement. This expansion aims to simplify the music selection process, making it more accessible and efficient for content creators.
<br/>
Additionally, YouTube is rolling out a new AI-dubbing tool called Aloud, integrated into YouTube Studio. This tool simplifies the process of generating AI-based voiceovers in different languages. Creators can easily review and incorporate these AI-generated dubs into their videos with just a click. Aloud is currently being tested with select creators and is expected to become more widely available next year.
<br/>
These announcements were made during YouTube's "Made on YouTube" live event, where the platform introduced various AI features, including a generative AI tool for Shorts, as well as the unveiling of a new creator app. These developments demonstrate YouTube's commitment to leveraging AI to enhance the content creation experience for its vast community of creators.
