US intelligence agencies have issued a concerning alert regarding foreign intelligence entities (FIEs) targeting the US space industry. The National Counterintelligence and Security Center (NCSC) released a bulletin warning of cyberattacks, espionage, and counter-space systems' use by FIEs to gather sensitive information on American space capabilities. The US space sector's strategic significance for the economy and national security has attracted FIE attention, with the global space economy projected to surpass $1 trillion by 2030. The NCSC bulletin emphasizes FIEs' multifaceted tactics, including cyberattacks and supply chain manipulation, granting them access to vital expertise.
<br/>
These infiltrations can disrupt supply chains and compromise critical aerospace components, exemplified by the 2021 SolarWinds breach targeting NASA. FIE activities pose threats to both commercial firms and national security by stealing intellectual property and targeting space infrastructure. The aftermath could lead to economic devastation for US space firms. The NCSC bulletin offers strategies to safeguard the sector, including vigilant anomaly logs, insider threat programs, collaboration across departments, identifying critical assets, and enhancing supply chain security.
<br/>
In light of this alert, it's imperative for the US to take robust measures to protect its space industry's foundation and prevent potential catastrophic consequences.

# ***Thailand Threatening Facebook Shutdown Over Scam Ads***
<br/>
Thailand's Minister of Digital Economy and Society, Chaiwut Thanakamanusorn, has threatened Facebook with severe actions due to poor ad filtering that has exposed users to costly scams. Minister Thanakamanusorn intends to take the case to a criminal court by the month's end and even calls for Facebook's closure in the nation. The minister accuses Facebook of inadequately screening fraudulent ads, which continue to proliferate despite the removal of over 5,000 such ads following requests from Facebook's parent company, Meta.
<br/>
These fake ads often feature seemingly legitimate financial experts promising high returns, leading unsuspecting users into financial frauds. Over 200,000 people in Thailand have reportedly fallen victim to online scams. The Thai government is amassing evidence to support its allegations and considering potential legal actions. The Ministry suggests that if proven, the court could target specific sites, accounts, or even the entire Facebook platform.
<br/>
Facebook is highly popular in Thailand with over 50 million users in a nation of 71 million. The susceptibility of the platform to online fraud is concerning, given reports of Thai scam victims losing over $285 million annually. In a short period from March to July 2023, internet fraud resulted in theft of $1.1 billion, and fraudulent investment schemes duped thousands, causing losses of $590 million. The Thai Cyber Crime Investigation Division highlighted the extent of financial damage caused by online scammers, involving tactics like job-related scams, lending, and investment frauds.

# ***Montana Seeks Judge Approval to Ban TikTok Amid Legal Challenge***
<br/>
<Image src="/us-china-internet-politics-tiktok.jpg" alt="" width={600} height={500} />
<br />
Montana is seeking approval from a federal judge to enforce its law banning new downloads of TikTok, a popular video-sharing app. This request coincides with an ongoing legal challenge filed by TikTok and content creators against the law. The law, introduced by Montana's Attorney General Austin Knudsen, is a response to concerns raised by the FBI and US Secretary of State about TikTok's Chinese ownership potentially granting access to sensitive US information or spreading pro-Beijing disinformation. The state's response argues that the law protects citizens from potential risks tied to TikTok's operations, comparing it to banning a cancer-causing radio that also transmits protected speech. They highlight the availability of alternative communication apps, downplaying the ban's impact on users.
<br/>
TikTok has defended itself by asserting its content moderation efforts and denying sharing user data with China. Critics, however, reference China's national intelligence law that mandates cooperation with the government in intelligence matters. Montana's law prohibits TikTok downloads within the state and imposes a $10,000 daily fine on entities offering access to the app. Individual users are exempt from these penalties. The dispute centers on concerns over national security and free speech rights, with TikTok's measures to safeguard users and Montana's intention to mitigate potential risks standing at odds. The outcome will impact both the future of the app and the broader conversation around technology, security, and personal freedoms.

# ***How EU's Digital Services Act is revolutionizing online platforms***
<br/>
<Image src="/rJzGhdvus_0_0_803_567_0_x-large.jpg" alt="" width={600} height={500} />
<br />
The tech sector is approaching a pivotal juncture as the implementation of the Digital Services Act (DSA) begins to reshape the landscape for major online platforms and services like Meta, Amazon, TikTok, Apple, and Google. This legislation, set to take effect primarily in the European Union (EU), will have far-reaching implications. Meta is introducing user challenges to content visibility decisions, Amazon will report illegal products and disclose more seller info, TikTok is enhancing its reporting system and curbing ads for young users, and Apple and Google are expected to modify their algorithmic recommendations.
<br/>
However, these changes primarily apply to the EU, as the DSA expands regulations on online platforms. The legislation mandates monitoring of illegal content, curbing certain advertising practices, and sharing information with authorities. The law initially focuses on 19 Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) and Very Large Online Search Engines (VLOSEs) with over 45 million active EU users, extending to all companies by February 2024. The upcoming Digital Markets Act (DMA) will further expand regulations.
<br/>
Although these regulations may resonate globally due to the EU's influence on regulatory trends, the immediate impact on tech giants in other regions might be limited. While the EU aims to rebalance authority and promote competition in the tech ecosystem, local adoption of similar regulations in places like Israel remains uncertain. The DSA and DMA could potentially turn Israel into a testing ground for actions regulated in Europe, given its lack of aligned regulations.
<br/>
The DSA is triggering significant changes in the tech sector, particularly within the EU, as major platforms adapt to new regulations aimed at promoting fairness, competition, and user protection. The potential global impact of these regulations is anticipated, but their direct influence beyond the EU remains uncertain.

# ***India's Chandrayaan-3 Is First Lander on Moon's South Pole: What's Next?***
<br/>
India's ISRO has accomplished a significant feat in lunar exploration by successfully landing the Chandrayaan-3 spacecraft's Vikram lander on the Moon's south pole. This achievement, occurring at 12:33 P.M. UTC on August 23, places India among the elite nations that have achieved successful lunar landings, including Russia, the United States, and China. India's landing is distinctive as it's the first to reach the far side of the moon, and it comes shortly after Russia's Luna-25 mission crashed during a similar attempt.
<br/>
The south pole landing is of immense importance due to its unique geological features and the potential presence of valuable resources like water ice. The successful landing is attributed to ISRO's adoption of a "failure-based design" approach, learning from the Chandrayaan-2 mission's 2019 lander crash. The upgraded guidance and control systems, alongside additional fuel for precise maneuvering, were crucial improvements.
<br/>
Chandrayaan-3's autonomous descent involved intricate coordination of engines and sensors through phases like rough braking, attitude hold, fine braking, and terminal descent. The landing was remarkably smooth, with vertical velocity well below 2 meters per second.
<br/>
This triumph holds promise for India's forthcoming lunar missions and global lunar exploration. The data collected by the lander and rover will deepen our understanding of the Moon's atmosphere, surface, and tectonics. Additionally, the discovery of water ice in the region has significant implications for future missions. This success opens doors for collaboration and future lunar missions, aligning with the Artemis Accords and offering India the chance to partner with other nations for advanced lunar exploration.
